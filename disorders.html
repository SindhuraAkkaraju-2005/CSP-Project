Understanding Genetic Disorders

Learn about what causes genetic disorders, how they occur, and their types

What Are Genetic Disorders?

Genetic disorders are diseases caused by changes or mutations in DNA. These changes affect the normal function of genes, leading to health problems.

How Do They Occur?

  • Inherited from parents: Passed down through families.
  • Mutations during reproduction: New errors in genes or chromosomes.
  • Can be caused by a single gene, multiple genes, or chromosomal abnormalities.

Types of Genetic Disorders

1. Single-Gene Disorders

Definition: Caused by a mutation in one specific gene.
Examples: Sickle Cell Anemia, Thalassemia, Cystic Fibrosis

2. Chromosomal Disorders

Definition: Caused by missing, extra, or damaged chromosomes.
Examples: Down Syndrome, Turner Syndrome, Klinefelter Syndrome

3. Multifactorial Disorders

Definition: Caused by a combination of genetic and environmental factors.
Examples: Diabetes, Heart Disease, Obesity, Breast Cancer
