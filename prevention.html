Prevention & Awareness

Understand how to reduce the risk of genetic disorders and stay informed

🧬 Importance of Awareness

Awareness is the first step to prevention. Understanding your family’s medical history and learning how genetics affect health can help you take proactive steps toward well-being.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Know Your Family History

  • Collect health records of parents, grandparents, siblings, and close relatives.
  • Identify patterns like diabetes, heart disease, cancer, or genetic syndromes.
  • Share this history with your doctor for better diagnosis and screening.

🧪 Genetic Counseling & Testing

  • Seek genetic counseling if you have a family history of inherited disorders.
  • Testing can detect carriers or inherited conditions before symptoms appear.
  • It is especially useful before planning a family or during pregnancy.

🍎 Healthy Lifestyle Choices

  • Maintain a balanced diet rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants.
  • Exercise regularly and avoid harmful habits like smoking or excessive drinking.
  • Manage stress with meditation, sleep, and mental health care.

🔍 Regular Screenings & Early Detection

  • Go for regular health checkups and screenings based on your genetic risk.
  • Conditions like cancer or diabetes are easier to manage if caught early.
  • Talk to your doctor about personalized screening schedules.

📢 Spread Awareness

  • Educate your family and community about inherited disorders.
  • Use social media or community drives to spread preventive knowledge.
  • Promote genetic literacy in schools and public health programs.
