üß¨ What Are Genes?

Genes are small sections of DNA that act like instruction manuals for your body. These instructions decide everything from your eye color and height to how your body functions. Every person has thousands of genes that are passed from their parents to them ‚Äî this is why you may share features with your mother, father, or grandparents.

Genes live inside structures called chromosomes, which are found in the nucleus of every cell in your body.

‚ö†Ô∏è What Are Genetic Disorders?

Genetic disorders are health conditions caused by abnormalities in your genes or chromosomes. Some of these are inherited from parents, while others happen due to sudden changes in genes (mutations) during cell division.

There are different types of genetic disorders based on how they occur:

  1. Single-Gene Disorders
    These occur when a single gene is mutated or missing.
    Example: Sickle Cell Anemia, Cystic Fibrosis, Thalassemia
  2. Chromosomal Disorders
    These occur due to missing, extra, or damaged chromosomes.
    Example: Down Syndrome (extra chromosome 21), Turner Syndrome (missing X chromosome in females)
  3. Multifactorial Disorders
    These are caused by a combination of multiple genes and environmental factors like diet, lifestyle, or pollution.
    Example: Diabetes, Heart Disease, Cancer

üß¨ How Do Genetic Disorders Occur?

Genetic disorders can happen in three major ways:

  • Inherited from Parents ‚Äì If one or both parents carry faulty genes, they can pass them to their children.
  • Spontaneous Mutations ‚Äì Sometimes genes get damaged or copied incorrectly during reproduction, even if the parents are healthy.
  • Chromosomal Abnormalities ‚Äì If a baby has too many or too few chromosomes, or if parts of chromosomes are broken or rearranged, this leads to disorders.
